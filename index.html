About me

The context behind the work.

I'm a Data Science undergraduate at Lake Forest College — concentration in Computer Science, minor in Economics and Sociology. I was born and raised with Arabic as my first language, completed the International Baccalaureate in Italy, and now work across humanitarian, civic, and analytical contexts in Chicago.

My work sits at the intersection of data, human systems, and language. I'm drawn to problems where the data is messy because the reality underneath it is complex: immigration, urban equity, community health, cross-cultural communication.

Outside of data work, I've served as a panel speaker for Médecins Sans Frontières, co-developed curriculum in International Humanitarian Law, and led fundraising for international student emergency support. These aren't side stories — they shape how I think about what data should be for.
